Following your communication, I took the opportunity to conduct a preliminary review of the Huaxin Future Holdings Group Co., Ltd. structure and its diversified business operations, including its focus on international trade, cross-border logistics, and overseas warehouse operations. This diligence is a standard practice at EUROCOMEX TRADE, as we seek to understand the full profile of our potential partners to ensure the best possible alignment of expectations and market strategy.
This initial research confirms a significant potential for a strategic alliance between our organizations. EUROCOMEX TRADE is a company with a long and successful history in the Brazilian market, backed by over two decades of experience with Chinese manufacturers, including serving as the exclusive representative for Shandong Shengli and Windmill in the industrial piping and valve sectors.
Our interest in your line of folding carts, handcarts, and transport tools is genuine, and we see a strong market fit in South America (via Brazil) and potentially in Europe (via our established branch in Portugal, where we have operated for nearly 30 years).
However, to successfully launch and sustain your brand in these new territories, we must move beyond a simple client-supplier relationship. The current proposal, centered on a catalog and FOB pricing, places 100% of the market risk and development burden on EUROCOMEX TRADE.
Given Huaxin's expertise in supply chain management, cross-border e-commerce, and overseas warehouse operations, we believe a true partnership should leverage these strengths to create a shared-risk, shared-reward model. A successful, long-term market entry requires:
Shared Responsibility: A commercial structure that divides the initial risks of market penetration.
Logistical Support: Leveraging Huaxin's logistics and warehouse capabilities to provide sample materials and facilitate initial market testing.
Strategic Alignment: A commitment to long-term market development in Brazil and Portugal, utilizing EUROCOMEX TRADE's established presence and market knowledge.
Mr. Zhan, if your intention is to find a dedicated, experienced partner willing to co-invest in the long-term success of your brand in the South American and European markets, we are open and eager to discuss a revised proposal that reflects this genuine strategic partnership.
If, however, the goal is simply to acquire another client under the traditional FOB model, we must respectfully decline this invitation. We thank you for the offer and wish you the best in your commercial endeavors.
